Latkes

Latkes are a form of potato pancake popular in every Jewish household, a grilled latke topped with sour cream or hot sauce can be a delectable side dish for any dinner.

Ingredients

  • 1 1/2 pound potatoes ( 3 to 4 potatoes)
  • 1/2 medium yellow onion, peeled and quartered
  • 1 large egg
  • 2 tablespoons matzo meal or unseasoned dry breadcrumbs
  • 1 teaspoon kosher salt
  • 1/8 teaspoon freshly ground black pepper
  • 1 cup canola oil or chicken schmaltz, or a combination of both
  • Sourcream and/or hot sauce for serving

Steps

  1. Heat the oven and prepare a baking sheet with a wire cooling rack, prepare a paper towel-lined baking pan to receive hot latkes.
  2. Grate the potatoes and onions using a large shredding blade on your food processor or grate using a box grater.
  3. Squeeze the potatoes and onions using a towel or cloth in order to absorb all of the liquid and starch.
  4. Mix the potato starch, egg, matzo, salt, and pepper with the potatoes and onion.
  5. Form latkes using a spatula measuring cup or your fingers into a flat, four inch patty.
  6. Fry the latkes in hot oil or schmaltz if using, fry each side until dark golden brown.
  7. Drain and serve the latkes by placing them on the paper towels.
